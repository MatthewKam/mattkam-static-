PROJECTS

WDI 5/6 Yearbook

Dinna Gonzales and I created a yearbook for our graduatin class at General Assembly's Web Development Immersive Program. It is a three month development bootcamp that does a deep dive into web dev. Dinna and I worked on this while doing our final projects. Long hours and late nights paid off in the end. I took profile pictures, group photos and videos. This was an awesome group. We all ended up being a big crazy family.

Technologies used include: HTML, CSS, Ruby on Rails, Javascript, Angular JS, Postgres, Jquery, Illustrator, Photoshop, iMovie, HandBrake, Foundation and deployed with Heroku.

Turtle Power! Yes, here it is folks, beat your kids(at tic tac toe), destroy your friends, show 'em who's boss! Make enemies!! Open up the app and wait for another player to do the same, then let the games begin.

Project One. Technologies used include: HTML, CSS, Javascript, AngularJS, Illustrator and deployed with Firebase.

StudiousCafe

Solving REAL first world problems. Ever find yourself looking for a place to study/read/do work only to find there are no seats available, no outlets or worse, no bathroom? From great struggle, comes glorious salvation. StudiousCafe was created to aid people in their quest to find better places to study by creating an open source platform, to check in and notify followers of gems in the city, to save you time so you can get back to the grind(s):)

Project Two (still under construction). Technologies used include: HTML, CSS, Ruby on Rails(including authentication), MongoDB ,Bootstrap, Illustrator and deployed with Heroku.

Blink

If anyone took a look at my bookmarks on Chrome, they'd see an endless list of bookmarks. Some of them are ambiguous and have been in my bookmarks folder for a long time. As a student in a classroom filled with people like me eagerly trying to learn, sharing these resources are sometimes like trying to find a needle in a haystack. Created out pure necessity for my fellow group members, Team Raad(Andrew Donovan, Stephanie Raad) and myself, Blink helps us organize our links/bookmarks publicly and searchable through tags, topics, and by user.

Project Three. Technologies used include: HTML, CSS, Ruby on Rails(including authentication), Foundation, Javascript, AngularJS(c.r.u.d.), Created and Consumed our own API, Postgres, Illustrator, used TDD and deployed with Heroku.

TrueGood

While solving first world problems is fun, the reason I got into this industry is to make the world a better place. TrueGood was created by Mike Duval and I to help those in need by connecting them with volunteers. As of now, due to security risks, we are more geared towards getting organizations(churches and businesses) involved in giving back to the community that they reside in. There are plenty of programs that clean streets and parks and build homes but we are more focused on people who are elderly or disabled. The concept is simple, residents/guardians posts jobs that they are unable to do and volunteers will reach out and help. It could be something as simple as fixing a lightbulb or as laborious as moving the lawn. People can get involved in anyway they see fit. A good deed brightens a dark world.

Project Four. Technologies used include: HTML, CSS, Ruby on Rails(including authentication), Foundation, Created and Consumed our own API, Javascript, AngularJS(c.r.u.d.), AngularJS(ui-routing), Skrllr.js, Postgres, Illustrator, used TDD and deployed with Heroku.

Spikenators

Ideas sometimes birth other ideas and you never know who might have that same idea. In my Web Development Course at General Assembly, I didnt get a chance to work with certain people because I was either doing my own thing or working with another group. Dinna Gonzales, Mary Keenan and I created Spikenators as a job board. A place where you can post your idea for an app and others in the class can sign up to partner with you and set a time to meet. It could be a mini project or a big project, but the point was to fill the need of many.

Technologies used include: HTML, CSS, Ruby on Rails, Postgres, Foundation, Illustrator and deployed with Heroku.

Queue It Up

In the last few weeks of my web development bootcamp, teachers time was scarce and their lines long to get help. Created mostly by Aarong Tsang, with a little help from me, we created a basic app to have our fellow classmates post their questions on our app and the teachers could then call the next one in line, instead of being bombarded by 50 students at once.

Technologies used include: HTML, CSS, Ruby on Rails, Javascript, Angular JS, Postgres, Foundation and deployed with Heroku.

PhotoHunt

Not yet finished, but the idea is simple. Aaron Tsang and I are creating an app for photo enthusiasts. Users will create groups, and in those groups one person creates a topic to take pictures of. At the end of the week, everyone votes on the best photo and the person who gets the most votes, chooses the next weeks topic. Each group has a moderator to keep it kosher. Each group will have its own chatroom.

Technologies going to use: HTML, CSS, Ruby on Rails, Javascript, Angular JS, Postgres, Jquery, Illustrator, Bootstrap and deploy with Heroku.
